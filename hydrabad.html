Charimar Gate

About Charminar

Charminar is one of Hyderabad's most famous landmarks, built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. This grand structure, with four towering minarets, is a symbol of Hyderabad's rich heritage and architectural brilliance.

The monument was constructed to commemorate the end of a deadly plague and is located in the heart of the old city. Surrounded by bustling markets, Charminar is a perfect blend of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring intricate carvings and arches.

Today, Charminar remains a major tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into the city's vibrant culture, history, and the famous Laad Bazaar, known for its traditional bangles and pearls.
