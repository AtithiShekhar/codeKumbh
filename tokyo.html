Tokyo Tower

About the Tokyo Tower

The Tokyo Tower is a striking communications and observation tower located in Minato, Tokyo, Japan. Inspired by the Eiffel Tower, it was completed in 1958 and stands at 333 meters (1,092 feet), making it one of Japan’s tallest structures. The tower was built to support television and radio broadcasting and symbolizes Japan’s post-war rebirth and modernization.

Painted in white and international orange for aviation safety, the Tokyo Tower is a remarkable blend of tradition and modernity. It has two observation decks: the Main Deck at 150 meters and the Top Deck at 250 meters, offering breathtaking panoramic views of Tokyo’s skyline and, on clear days, even Mount Fuji.

More than just a broadcasting tower, it has become a cultural and tourist landmark, attracting over 3 million visitors annually. The base of the tower, known as FootTown, houses museums, shops, and restaurants, making it a lively entertainment hub. At night, the tower is illuminated with dazzling lights, enhancing its charm. Today, the Tokyo Tower remains an enduring symbol of Tokyo’s progress and innovation, continuing to captivate visitors from around the world.
